<details>
  <summary><h2>Product Description</h2></summary>

Every year, millions of people relocate for work, education, family, or a better quality of life. However, traditional real estate platforms mainly focus on the physical features of a property, such as price, square meters, room count, or building age. They often fail to answer one of the most important questions in the decision-making process:

**“What is it actually like to live in this neighborhood?”**

**OURNB** is an AI-powered, map-based neighborhood intelligence platform designed to reduce this information gap in relocation and real estate decisions. Instead of evaluating only the house itself, OURNB helps users understand the social, logistical, and environmental character of a neighborhood before moving there.

The platform allows users to explore neighborhoods through an interactive map interface and view a dynamic **Neighborhood Life Score** based on multiple factors such as transportation accessibility, social venue density, green areas, walkability, noise level, and daily life convenience. In addition to numerical scores, OURNB uses Large Language Models (LLMs) to analyze scattered user experiences and transform them into structured insights, summaries, advantages, disadvantages, and semantic tags.

A key feature of the platform is the **AI Neighborhood Assistant**, which interprets users’ lifestyle preferences in natural language. For example, users can describe themselves as a remote worker, student, pet owner, parent, or someone looking for a quiet and social area. The assistant then compares these preferences with neighborhood scores and semantic insights to recommend the most suitable locations.

At the end of the day, OURNB is not just a real estate search tool. It is a decision-support platform that helps people understand the life beyond the walls of a house. Because people do not only choose a home; they choose the streets, routines, sounds, opportunities, and lifestyle surrounding it.

<details>
  <summary><h2>Product Features</h2></summary>

### 🗺️ 1. Map-Based Neighborhood Discovery

OURNB provides a mobile-first map interface where users can explore neighborhoods visually.  
Unlike traditional map applications that mainly focus on businesses, restaurants, cafés, or landmarks, OURNB focuses on **neighborhood-level living experiences**.

Users can tap on a neighborhood, street, or selected area on the map to view:

- Neighborhood overview
- User comments
- AI-generated summaries
- Lifestyle compatibility score
- Key advantages and disadvantages
- Category-based neighborhood insights

This feature helps users understand the atmosphere of an area before physically visiting or moving there.

---

### 🏘️ 2. Neighborhood Review System

Users can share their real-life experiences about neighborhoods, streets, and local living conditions.

Example review topics include:

- Transportation access
- Noise level
- Parking problems
- Walkability
- Social life
- Green areas
- Internet infrastructure
- Pet-friendliness
- Student suitability
- Family suitability
- Daily life convenience

Instead of reviewing only individual places like cafés or restaurants, users can review the **overall living experience of a neighborhood**.

---

### ⭐ 3. Neighborhood Life Score

Each neighborhood is evaluated through a dynamic **Neighborhood Life Score**.

The score is calculated based on multiple indicators such as:

- Public transportation accessibility
- Social venue density
- Green area availability
- Walkability
- Daily needs accessibility
- Noise and calmness level
- Internet and infrastructure suitability
- User experience signals

The goal is not to label a neighborhood as simply “good” or “bad,” but to help users understand whether it matches their personal lifestyle.

---

### 🤖 4. AI-Generated Neighborhood Summary

OURNB uses Large Language Models to analyze user-generated comments and transform them into short, meaningful summaries.

Instead of reading dozens of individual comments, users can quickly understand the general opinion about a neighborhood.

Example AI summary:

> “This neighborhood is frequently praised for its public transportation access, cafés, and walkable streets. However, users often mention parking problems and increased noise levels near the main road.”

This feature saves time and turns scattered local opinions into structured insights.

---

### 🧠 5. AI Neighborhood Assistant

The AI Neighborhood Assistant helps users find neighborhoods that match their lifestyle needs.

Users can describe what they are looking for in natural language.

Example prompts:

- “I work remotely and need a quiet neighborhood with good internet.”
- “I am a student and want an affordable area close to public transportation.”
- “I have a dog and need parks nearby.”
- “I want a social area but not too noisy at night.”

The assistant analyzes the user’s preferences and compares them with neighborhood scores, reviews, and semantic tags to suggest suitable areas.

---

### 🏷️ 6. Semantic Neighborhood Tags

OURNB automatically extracts meaningful tags from neighborhood reviews and data.

Example tags:

- `#RemoteWorkFriendly`
- `#GoodPublicTransport`
- `#QuietStreets`
- `#ParkingProblem`
- `#PetFriendly`
- `#StudentFriendly`
- `#FamilyFriendly`
- `#SocialAreaDense`
- `#Walkable`
- `#GreenAreaNearby`

These tags help users quickly understand the strongest and weakest aspects of each neighborhood.

---

### 📊 7. Neighborhood Comparison

Users can compare multiple neighborhoods based on their priorities.

For example, a user can compare three neighborhoods according to:

- Transportation
- Quietness
- Social life
- Green areas
- Parking
- Remote-work suitability
- Family-friendliness

This feature supports users who are deciding between several possible locations before renting, buying, or relocating.

---

### 🔍 8. Personalized Lifestyle Matching

OURNB does not recommend the same neighborhood to every user.

The platform considers different lifestyle profiles, such as:

- Remote worker
- Student
- Young professional
- Family with children
- Pet owner
- Digital nomad
- First-time home buyer

Based on these profiles, the system calculates how suitable a neighborhood is for the user’s needs.

---

### 🛡️ 9. Review Moderation and Trust Layer

Neighborhood reviews can be subjective or manipulative.  
To reduce misinformation, spam, and biased comments, OURNB includes a moderation layer.

The moderation system can help detect:

- Fake or repetitive reviews
- Toxic language
- Extremely biased comments
- Manipulative neighborhood descriptions
- Spam-like content

This improves the reliability of neighborhood insights.

---

### 📍 10. Pilot Neighborhood MVP

For the first version, OURNB will focus on selected pilot neighborhoods instead of covering an entire city.

This allows the team to:

- Test the product with a realistic dataset
- Build a strong demo experience
- Improve the scoring logic
- Validate the AI-generated summaries
- Collect user feedback faster

The MVP will prioritize quality and clarity over large-scale geographic coverage.

<details>
  <summary><h2>Target Audience</h2></summary>

### 1. Remote Relocators — Young Professionals Moving to a New City

This group includes young professionals between the ages of 24 and 35 who move to a new city or district due to work opportunities, career changes, education, or rotation programs. They are often software developers, engineers, finance professionals, digital marketers, or other white-collar workers.

Their main challenge is the lack of reliable local knowledge. They usually do not have enough time to physically explore every street, and they may not know people who can provide objective insights about the area.

For this audience, **OURNB** provides a digital neighborhood guide that helps them understand transportation access, social life, internet infrastructure, walking comfort, and overall livability before moving.

---

### 2. Location-Independent Workers — Digital Nomads and Remote Workers

This group includes freelancers, remote employees, and digital nomads who can work from anywhere and often relocate for better lifestyle quality, lower living costs, or seasonal preferences.

For them, a home is not only a place to live; it is also their working environment. Therefore, they care about factors such as:

- Quietness during working hours
- Stable internet infrastructure
- Nearby cafés and coworking-friendly places
- Walkable daily routines
- Sports and social facilities
- Environmental comfort

**OURNB** helps this audience identify neighborhoods that match their work-life balance and daily routines.

---

### 3. Conscious Home Buyers — First-Time Buyers and Young Families

This group includes people between the ages of 28 and 40 who are planning to buy a home for long-term living or investment. They may be newly married, starting a family, or planning a major financial decision.

Unlike short-term renters, this audience has a lower tolerance for making the wrong location decision. They need reliable and independent information about the neighborhood’s quality of life, future potential, social environment, green spaces, schools, transportation, and daily convenience.

**OURNB** helps them reduce investment risk by providing a data-driven neighborhood evaluation instead of relying only on subjective real estate claims.

---

## Sprint Notes

During Sprint 1, the team focused on product discovery, idea validation, task distribution, backlog planning, and technology decisions.

At the beginning of the sprint, the team held an initial meeting to discuss the product idea and the overall direction of the project. During this meeting, the first product idea was reviewed and evaluated in terms of problem-solution fit, MVP feasibility, technical scope, and demo potential.

After discussion, the team decided to make a product pivot. Several new ideas were proposed, compared, and evaluated. As a result, the team selected **OURNB** as the final product idea.

**OURNB** was defined as a mobile-first, AI-powered neighborhood intelligence application. The main goal of the product is to help users understand what it is actually like to live in a neighborhood before moving, renting, buying, or investing in a new location.

After the product idea was finalized, responsibilities were distributed among team members. Product features, target audience, market research, SWOT analysis, backlog structure, sprint documentation, and product planning tasks were assigned.

Throughout the week, Daily Scrum updates were carried out through Slack messages. Team members shared their progress, discussed blockers, and coordinated responsibilities. In addition, the team discussed which technologies would be suitable for the MVP and completed the sprint by deciding on the initial technical direction.

## Backlog Management and User Story Selection

During Sprint 1, the product backlog was reorganized according to the new product direction after the team decided to pivot to **OURNB**.

The team first clarified the main user journey of the application:

1. The user opens the mobile application.
2. The user explores neighborhoods on a map.
3. The user selects a neighborhood.
4. The user views neighborhood-level comments, scores, and insights.
5. The system generates an AI-powered summary of the selected neighborhood.
6. The user receives recommendations based on lifestyle preferences.

Based on this journey, the team selected the first user stories for the MVP. The selected stories were prioritized according to demo value, technical feasibility, connection to the core problem, and AI/data science contribution.

### Selected User Stories for Sprint 1

| Priority | User Story | Description | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| High | As a user, I want to explore neighborhoods on a map so that I can visually understand different areas. | Map-based neighborhood discovery | Planned |
| High | As a user, I want to view neighborhood details so that I can understand the living conditions of an area. | Neighborhood detail card | Planned |
| High | As a user, I want to read neighborhood comments so that I can learn from real experiences. | Neighborhood review system | Planned |
| High | As a user, I want to see a Neighborhood Life Score so that I can compare different areas more easily. | Neighborhood scoring system | Planned |
| Medium | As a user, I want to see AI-generated summaries so that I can understand the overall opinion without reading every comment. | LLM-powered neighborhood summary | Planned |
| Medium | As a user, I want to receive neighborhood recommendations based on my lifestyle so that I can find suitable locations faster. | AI Neighborhood Assistant | Planned |
| Low | As an admin, I want to moderate comments so that spam, fake, or manipulative reviews can be controlled. | Review moderation system | Backlog |

### Backlog Selection Criteria

The team selected backlog items using the following criteria:

- Relevance to the MVP
- Connection to the main user problem
- Technical feasibility within the bootcamp timeline
- Contribution of AI and data science components
- Visual impact for the final demo
- Dependency between features
- Value for the target audience

The highest priority was given to features that clearly demonstrate the core value of OURNB: map-based neighborhood exploration, neighborhood comments, life score, AI-generated summaries, and lifestyle-based recommendations.

## Sprint Review

During the Sprint Review, the team discussed the progress made during Sprint 1 and evaluated the current product direction.

Since the product idea changed during this sprint, the main focus of the review was the product pivot, the newly selected idea, and the MVP scope. The team agreed that **OURNB** has a clearer problem-solution fit and stronger demo potential.

The team reviewed the assigned tasks, including product description, target audience, product features, SWOT analysis, backlog structure, and technology decisions. The team also discussed how the product should continue in Sprint 2.
